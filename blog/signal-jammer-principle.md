## 1. The Core Principle: From Physical Shielding to Electronic Warfare

### Metal Shielding (Faraday Cage)
The simplest way to block a signal is through **Electrostatic Shielding**. By wrapping a phone in aluminum foil or placing it in a sealed metal box, electromagnetic waves are prevented from penetrating the metal surface. This is known as the "Faraday Cage" effect.

However, since turning classrooms into metal vaults is impractical, a more active method is used: **Frequency Sweeping Interference**.

### Electronic Interference: Creating "Radio Noise"
A modern **Signal Jammer** doesn't actually "block" signals in the physical sense. Instead, it "drowns" them out.

Think of it this way: communication between a phone and a base station is like a conversation between two people. A jammer acts like a loudspeaker right next to them, playing loud "static" or "noise." When the noise intensity exceeds the signal, the phone cannot extract meaningful data, leading to a "No Service" status.
